Methods Report: The Oracle Multiplex

Architecture: Inductive Multiplex Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)

Task: Prequential Cold-Start Protein-Ligand Binding Affinity Prediction ($pIC_{50}$)

1. The Core Problem: The "Information Base" vs. The "Blindfold"

Standard Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) fail in drug discovery because they assume a static graph where all labels are known. In the real world, proteins arrive one by one. When a new protein arrives, it has zero known binding edges.

The Oracle Multiplex solves this by treating the protein corpus not as a training set, but as a Living Information Base. We use a Prequential (Test-then-Train) Protocol to ensure that the model is evaluated on its ability to "leak" information from similar labeled proteins into a cold-start target.

2. Phase 1: The Vertical Pillar & Prequential Stream

The data is structured as a Multiplex Graph with two primary "floors" of protein-protein similarity:

The Form Floor (Structural): Based on CATH structural folds and ESM-2 embeddings.

The Role Floor (Functional): Based on shared Gene Ontology (GO) terms.

The Streaming Protocol

Arrival: A target protein $P_{target}$ enters the stream.

Consultation: The MultiplexPillarSampler fetches the "Vertical Pillar"—the structural and functional neighbors of $P_{target}$ and their known binding histories.

Deployment: The model predicts affinities for a set of Query Drugs while strictly blindfolded to $P_{target}$'s own labels.

Revelation: A small batch of Support Edges (ground truth) is revealed.

Evolution: The model trains on the support edges and adds them to the global Information Base for future proteins to use.

3. Phase 2: The Inductive Leak (Multiplex Smoothing)

We do not average neighbor affinities. Instead, we perform Chemical Footprinting.
For every neighbor $P_{n}$ of the target, we construct a Binding Preference Vector $V_{pref}$ by projecting its known drug interactions into the MolCLR feature space:


$$V_{pref} = \sum (\text{Drug\_Features} \times (pIC_{50} - \text{Baseline}))$$

The Commonality Filter

A cross-attention mechanism determines which neighbors "matter."

If $P_{target}$ and $P_{n}$ share a rare structural pocket, the attention weight $\alpha$ is high.

If they share a generic functional term but have different structures, $\alpha$ is low.
The result is $Z_{refined}$, a protein latent representation that has "absorbed" the specific chemical likes and dislikes of its most relevant neighbors.

4. Phase 3: The Trust-Aware Routing (The Gate)

The model uses a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE). However, instead of a standard gate, we use a Trust-Aware Meta-Gate.
The Router calculates the Variance of the neighbors on each multiplex floor:

Low Variance = High Trust: Neighbors agree on what drugs bind; the signal is clean.

High Variance = Low Trust: Neighbors disagree; the signal is noisy.

The Gate looks at $[Z_{refined} \parallel \text{Trust}_{form} \parallel \text{Trust}_{role}]$ and routes the protein to one of $K$ specialized Expert Scorers. Each Expert acts as a different "Physics Engine," specializing in specific types of protein-ligand interactions (e.g., rigid-pocket vs. allosteric).

5. Phase 4 & 5: Explanation-Based Learning (EBL)

To prevent "Expert Collapse" (where the model only uses one expert), we implement Explanation-Based Learning (EBL).
After the Support Labels are revealed, the model performs an Autopsy:

It calculates which of the $K$ experts would have been the most accurate for this specific protein.

It creates an "Oracle Distribution"—a target routing policy.

It penalizes the Gate (via Cross-Entropy) for not picking the best expert.

This forces the Gate to evolve its "Trust Policy," learning over time which biological signals are reliable and which are noise.

6. Summary of Innovation

Inductive: The model never "sees" the target protein's labels during evaluation; it must infer them from the topology.

Multiplex: It differentiates between structural similarity and functional similarity.

Dynamic: The Information Base grows with every episode, simulating a laboratory that gets smarter with every experiment.
